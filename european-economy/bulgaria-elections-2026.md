# Bulgaria Elections 2026: Toward a Pro-Russia Government?

## Context: Bulgaria's Political Crisis
- Bulgaria has held **8 parliamentary elections since 2021** — a record of political fragmentation in an EU member state
- Each election produced weak coalition governments with short lifespans
- The most recent government fell after large protests in December 2025
- Voter turnout in the last 4 elections: consistently **below 40%** — deep civic disengagement and institutional distrust

## Expected Outcome
- **Bulgaria Progressista** coalition (formed late 2025 by former President **Rumen Radev**) expected to win
- Radev expected to become the next prime minister

## Radev's Positions
- Populist, with explicit **pro-Russia leanings**:
  - Proposed a **referendum on adopting the euro** — many Bulgarians are skeptical of eurozone entry
  - Critical of EU/NATO **aid to Ukraine**
  - Against maintaining **EU sanctions on Russia**
- Would make Bulgaria the new pro-Russia dissident within the EU, at the exact moment Hungary is pivoting pro-European under Magyar

## Significance for the EU
- Potential new internal veto player on Russia sanctions, Ukraine support votes, and cohesion
- Bulgaria is smaller than Hungary in economic and political weight, but is strategically located (Black Sea, NATO flank) and its dysfunction creates ongoing vulnerabilities
- The sequencing is notable: the EU loses Orbán's obstruction just as it may gain Radev's

## Key Takeaways
- Bulgaria risks installing a pro-Russia government precisely as Hungary pivots pro-EU — partially offsetting Magyar's geopolitical shift
- 8 elections in 5 years reflect a failed political system; low turnout amplifies populist and protest votes
- Radev's euro referendum proposal is the most direct challenge to Bulgaria's EU integration path
- Watch: will the EU adopt the same rule-of-law fund-freezing strategy it used on Hungary if Radev governs against EU principles?
