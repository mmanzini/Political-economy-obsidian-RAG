# European Stagflation Risk

## EU Commission Warnings

- Commissioner Paolo Gentiloni: **"A stagflationary shock is arriving for Europe"**
- New European forecasts (mid-May) will downgrade growth
- **If energy costs return to pre-war levels by year-end**: growth impact -0.2–0.4%, inflation impact +1%
- **If energy decline is slower**: growth impact -0.6%, inflation impact +1.5%
- On energy windfall tax: must be coordinated across all countries; compensation measures must not weigh on public accounts
- Stability Pact derogation "only possible in case of severe recession"

- **Italy** (Min. Urso): preparing incentives for commercial vehicles in specific categories

- **BPER / Banca Popolare di Sondrio**: merger deed effective from 20 April

## Excise Tax Debate

- EU warned Italy and other countries: cutting excise duties on fuel is counterproductive
- It increases inflation, stimulates demand for a scarce good, disproportionately helps wealthier taxpayers, and creates fiscal holes

## Italy

- PM said in Parliament: "alongside farmers, not on barricades"; no government reshuffle
- Called for a Stability Pact that is "not stupid" — should not be taboo if crisis continues
- Italy recorded 7.3% deficit in 2023 — would not allow exit from excessive deficit procedures
- Minister Giorgetti: waiting for Eurostat estimates (third decade of April); GDP estimates to be revised due to war; urged suspension of TSO until prices realign
- Milan most resilient European market (held at -0.5% while Frankfurt fell >1%)
- 10-year BTP yield: 3.70% (falling); spread at 78 basis points; bond yield above 3%

## European Markets

- Frankfurt: >1% decline (weak industrial production, trade surplus data)
- Milan: held at -0.5% — most resilient
- Utilities performed well

## Key Takeaways

- EU is warning of a stagflationary shock with growth downgrades coming in May
- Excise duty cuts are a politically attractive but economically counterproductive response
- Italy's fiscal position (7.3% deficit) limits its room to manoeuvre within the Stability Pact
- Hungary's election could be a turning point for EU cohesion and fund allocation
