# Hungary Elections 2026

## Election Results (12 April 2026)

- **Tisza** (Péter Magyar): **141 seats** out of 199 — a supermajority (>133 needed for two-thirds); note: the preliminary election-night count was 138, revised to 141 after diaspora votes were tallied (Bloomberg, 2026-04-18)
- **Fidesz** (Viktor Orbán): **55 seats**
- **Mi Hazánk Mozgalom** (far-right): **6 seats**
- Turnout: **>78%** — highest in post-communist Hungarian history (previous record: 73.5% in 2002; 2022 was 69%)

## Significance

- Orbán's **first defeat after 16 years** in power (PM since 2010)
- He acknowledged the result publicly: called it "painful but clear" and congratulated Magyar by phone
- Massive street celebrations in Budapest

## Péter Magyar and Tisza

- Conservative politician; many Tisza members are **former Fidesz** figures
- Campaign focused on two themes:
  - **Corruption** within Fidesz leadership
  - **Institutional capture** — Fidesz's control over judiciary, media, electoral system, and public finances
- Several opposition parties withdrew to consolidate votes behind Magyar
- Magyar's supermajority allows him to modify "cardinal laws" (the 2011 reform that extended two-thirds requirements to judiciary, elections, media, family policy, church-state relations, etc.)
- However, courts still led by Orbán allies may block legislation

## Foreign Policy Implications

- Orbán was one of the EU's most **pro-Russian** and **anti-Ukraine** leaders — frequently blocked EU aid and financing to Ukraine
- Both Russia and the US had supported Orbán as a friendly government inside the EU
- Magyar is considered **more pro-European** and less pro-Russia, but has avoided strong foreign policy commitments so far
- His actual positions remain unclear

## EU Impact

- Could unlock **~€17B in frozen EU funds** (previously withheld over rule-of-law concerns; total outstanding across all withheld instruments estimated at ~€35B — see [[hungary-post-election-priorities]])
- Removes a major veto player on EU decisions regarding Ukraine support
- Potentially shifts EU internal dynamics on cohesion and foreign policy

## Key Takeaways

- Orbán's 16-year grip on Hungary ended with a historic supermajority defeat
- Record turnout (>78%) and opposition consolidation behind Magyar drove the result
- Magyar's supermajority enables constitutional-level reforms, though Orbán-allied courts may resist
- The geopolitical shift could be significant: reduced Russian influence within the EU and unblocked Ukraine support
