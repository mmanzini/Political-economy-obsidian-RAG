# Hungary Post-Election: Magyar's Priorities

## Government Formation Timeline
- Orbán remains in office until the new parliament convenes and votes confidence in the new government
- Expected transition: around **mid-May 2026**
- Orbán called Magyar to congratulate him; transition appears orderly

## Magyar's Stated Priorities

### Dismantling Orbán's System
- Remove Orbán loyalists from all positions of institutional power
- Introduce a **retroactive two-term limit for prime ministers** — this would legally bar Orbán from returning to power
- Constitutional changes are possible with a 2/3 majority (which Tisza holds); however courts remain dominated by Orbán-aligned judges
  - Option A: Constitutional Court (Fidesz-aligned) blocks legislation
  - Option B: Magyar amends the constitution itself
- After meeting President Sulyok Tamás (Fidesz): Magyar publicly called him unfit to be a "moral authority," demanded his resignation; hinted at impeachment proceedings if refused (requires 2/3 majority + Constitutional Court or constitution change)

### EU Frozen Funds (~€35B)
- The EU withheld funds over Orbán's systematic rule-of-law violations
- Earlier reporting cited ~€17B (cohesion/structural tranches); total outstanding across all withheld instruments: **~€35B** (includes Recovery and Resilience Facility funds)
- Hungary's economy is in poor shape; unlocking these funds is Magyar's most urgent economic priority
- The EU is expected to release funds once the transition is certified

### Druzhba Pipeline and Ukraine Rapprochement
- The Druzhba oil pipeline (Russian crude through Ukraine to Hungary) was damaged by Russian bombardment
- Orbán accused Zelensky of deliberately not repairing it, using this as pretext to block a **€90B EU loan to Ukraine**
- Zelensky (post-Magyar victory): committed to repairing the pipeline by **end of April 2026**
- Magyar asked Orbán to approve the Ukraine loan as soon as the pipeline is repaired

## Geopolitical Implications
- Orbán was the EU's primary internal enabler of Russian influence; his removal restructures EU dynamics on Ukraine, Russia, and cohesion
- Ukraine loan approval now unblocked once transition is complete
- Hungary pivots from pro-Russia to pro-EU alignment

## Key Takeaways
- Magyar holds supermajority power but faces Orbán-packed courts as the main institutional obstacle to reform
- €35B in EU funds is the economic prize and top priority
- The Druzhba pipeline repair is an early test of new Hungary-Ukraine relations and of Magyar's ability to deliver tangible change quickly
- The retroactive two-term limit is constitutionally feasible but will be legally contested
