# EU Marginalized in the Middle East Crisis

## The Core Reality
- Day 48 of the conflict (as of 2026-04-16): the EU and its member states have had no meaningful role in war outcomes
- **The war is decided by the US, Israel, and Iran** — Europe is not at the table
- EU diplomatic initiatives described as "slow and tied to a world order that no longer exists"

## What the EU Is Doing
- **António Costa** (President of the European Council): visited Gulf state leaders in mid-April 2026
- **Kaja Kallas** (High Representative for Foreign Affairs): visited UAE; attended UN in New York on April 13
- Kallas statement: EU supports a "strong international coalition for maritime security" — undefined
- France and Germany have offered to participate in a Hormuz security operation — but only after the war ends
- EU has developed a post-war Hormuz plan that **explicitly excludes the US and Israel**
- EU is also developing a **NATO contingency plan** for a scenario where the US withdraws from the alliance

## The Cost to Europe
- Since the conflict started (44 days): EU member states paid **€22B+ more** for fossil fuel imports
- Ireland: protests and fuel shortages; farmers and hauliers blocked the country's only oil refinery (Cork) for 5 days; government cut fuel excise taxes; motion of no confidence defeated
- BCE forecast: in the adverse scenario, euro zone growth cut to **1.4%** in 2026 and **1.2%** in 2027

## The Strategic Implication
- EU post-war planning that excludes the US reflects a quiet acknowledgment that the transatlantic relationship may be structurally changing
- European defense spending and autonomy discussions accelerating
- NATO Secretary General Rutte: "a NATO less dependent on the US is not necessarily a bad thing"
- The EU cannot yet act as an independent security actor; it is doing diplomacy as a signal of relevance, not a mechanism of influence

## Key Takeaways
- The EU has no leverage or agency in the active conflict — purely in observer mode
- €22B in extra energy costs over 44 days is the measurable price of EU exposure to a war it cannot influence
- Post-war planning excluding the US signals a strategic decoupling in progress, if not yet complete
- The EU is caught between the old rules-based order (its native language) and the new predatory order (see [[predatory-world-order]]) — and has no tools for the latter
