# Underground Banking and Crypto Critique

*Source: "Fuori da Qui" podcast Ep.127 — Banche (Simone Pirani, Coranews)*

## Underground Banking Networks

- **Fei Qian** ("flying money") in China, **Hawala** in the Islamic world — informal value-transfer systems dating back to the 7th–9th century (Silk Road origins)
- Operate on **radical mutual trust**: betrayal means permanent expulsion from the network
- No physical money crosses borders; settlement is via matched local disbursements and secret codes (often sent via WeChat)
- Estimated global volume: **hundreds of billions of dollars/year**, mostly innocent immigrant remittances

### Case Study: Ma Mo (Guangzhou)

- Ran an unlicensed currency exchange in a wholesale market
- Served foreign buyers needing CNY and Chinese merchants receiving foreign currency
- Convicted of "illegal business operations" — 3 years in prison + heavy financial penalty
- Chinese government views underground banks as threats to **national financial security** and macroeconomic stability

### Sinaloa Cartel–Chinese Student Pipeline

- Chinese families need USD to pay US tuition (e.g., ~$70K/yr at NYU Stern) but face a **$50,000/yr capital control limit**
- Sinaloa Cartel has excess dirty USD from fentanyl sales in the US
- The circuit:
  1. Cartel delivers dirty dollars to Chinese brokers in the US
  2. Brokers give dollars to Chinese students for tuition
  3. Partners in China credit equivalent CNY to cartel-linked accounts
  4. Cartel uses CNY to **buy fentanyl precursor chemicals** from Chinese manufacturers
  5. Chinese companies purchase goods from cartel-controlled Mexican firms → clean pesos
- No money physically crosses borders; no international wire transfers

## Cryptocurrency: Failed Promises

- Satoshi Nakamoto (2009): Bitcoin designed as a **"trustless" currency** — no intermediaries, no central banks
- Reality:
  - Extreme volatility made Bitcoin unusable as a currency → became a **speculative asset**
  - Stablecoins sought stability but invited **regulatory absorption** (same AML obligations as banks)
  - The blockchain is a **public ledger** — by definition the most traceable payment system ever created
  - Government agencies + digital forensics firms (e.g., Chainalysis) actively monitor blockchain transactions
- **Irony**: technology designed for anonymity produced the most easily surveilled financial system in history

## Cyber-Libertarian Ideology (David Golumbia's critique)

- Silicon Valley "freedom" rhetoric is rooted in **American extreme libertarianism**
- Terms like "open," "innovation," "efficiency" have been **stripped of progressive meaning** → used to justify deregulation
- "Open financial system" = absence of institutional oversight, not reduction of inequality
- Framing social problems as engineering problems **delegitimises political engagement**
- Philip Mirowski's **matryoshka metaphor**: outer shell of civic hackers and transparency attracts activists; inner core pursues **dismantling state regulation** for corporate interests
- Bitcoin's scarcity model derives from theories of the **John Birch Society** (American conservative org)
- Crypto communities function as **ideological echo chambers** that normalise far-right economic theories under the guise of technological innovation
- Progressive supporters of blockchain unknowingly helped **concentrate power** in Wall Street investors and unaccountable oligarchies

## The Global Financial Surveillance Machine

- The official financial system is a "colossal factory" — every digital payment is scanned, verified, monitored
- Banks spend **~$200B/year** on compliance and financial crime prevention
- **20% of banking workforce** dedicated to investigation and compliance
- Miles Kellerman (Leiden University): this infrastructure makes money "legible to the state"

## The Double Bind

- **Digital illusion** (crypto): promises emancipation, delivers the most transparent surveillance cage + concentrates wealth
- **Analog illusion** (underground banking): genuinely evades state detection, but inevitably becomes a vehicle for drug trafficking and terrorism (Al-Qaeda, Sinaloa)
- No true escape route: any attempt to evade control merely shifts power into darker hands

## Key Takeaways

- Ancient informal financial networks (Hawala, Fei Qian) remain the only systems genuinely opaque to state surveillance — not crypto
- Cryptocurrency's blockchain is inherently traceable and has been absorbed into the regulated financial system
- The cyber-libertarian ideology behind crypto is a political project favouring deregulation and corporate power, not citizen emancipation
- Underground finance enables both innocent remittances and serious crime — there is no clean escape from state financial surveillance
