# Africa and the Global Anti-Fascist War: Decolonising WWII Memory

*Source: "Fuori da Qui" podcast (Coranews), Ep. "Precisione" (Simone Pirani, 2026-04-25). Drawing on Tricontinental journal, "Rethinking Resistance: Revolts, Violence in African History" (eds. Abbao/Gigoo), and academic research on Ethiopian resistance (Aregawi Bere; Tessfa Abbao & Gigoo).*

## The Core Thesis: Africa Was Not the Periphery

- Conventional WWII narrative: European and North Atlantic; Africa reduced to an exotic theatre for Rommel vs Montgomery
- The revisionist claim: **Africa was the propulsive centre** — the material reservoir and first battlefield of global anti-fascism without which Allied victory was impossible
- Proposed terminological shift: call it the **"global anti-fascist war"** rather than the "Second World War" — to acknowledge African struggles and decolonise historical memory

## The Thiaroye Massacre (Senegal, 1 December 1944)

- African tirailleurs (tirailleur sénégalais) returning from European fronts — they had fought to liberate France from fascism
- Arrived at Camp de Thiaroye for demobilisation; demanded the full pay owed, including prison camp arrears
- The French state attempted to pay them at a devalued exchange rate — stealing wages from those who had saved the republic
- French colonial forces responded with armoured vehicles and heavy machine guns against unarmed men at 5:30 AM during morning prayers
- Official French figure: 35 dead (unchanged for decades); historical research: **hundreds killed, possibly 300–400**; bodies buried in unmarked mass graves to erase the memory
- Film: *Camp de Thiaroye* (1988, Ousmane Sembène) — the refusal of the old man to shake the French general's hand: "in that single restrained gesture, Sembène captures the moral balance sheet of the colonial empire"

## The Algerian Massacres (8 May 1945)

- While Europe celebrated VE Day (liberation from fascism), Algerian demonstrators marching for independence were massacred by French troops
- Thousands killed — same day, same republic, celebrating freedom
- This is not an unfortunate coincidence: it is a **structural contradiction** — the fascist logic Europe declared defeated at home continued uninterrupted in the colonies
- The colonial logic did not die with the Third Reich; it **outlived it**

## Africa's Material Contribution

- **Soldiers**: millions of African troops fought on European fronts; the tirailleurs, the Malagasy, the Algerian tirailleurs
- **Agriculture**: forced requisition of food supplies in French West Africa caused devastating famines — food taken from producers to feed a war fought thousands of kilometres away
- **Uranium**: the atomic bombs dropped on Hiroshima and Nagasaki were built from uranium mined at **Shinkolobwe**, Belgian Congo
  - Mined by African workers subjected to extreme exploitation, with no radiation protection
  - No memorial, no recognition — the Congolese miners' names appear nowhere in the victory celebrations
- **The "second occupation"**: wartime colonial administrations intensified, not loosened, their grip — a systematic extraction that drained the continent

## Ethiopia: First Line of Global Anti-Fascism

- **October 1935**: Fascist Italy (Badoglio, Graziani) invaded Ethiopia under Mussolini — European powers looked away via League of Nations inaction
- **The "Italiani brava gente" myth dismantled**: Italian colonial forces used poison gas on soldiers and civilians, conducted summary executions, decapitations as trophies
  - The Addis Ababa reprisals after the assassination attempt on Graziani: three days of massacre
  - The massacre of monks and pilgrims at Debre Libanos (hundreds killed for suspected rebel support)
  - This is the reality the myth of "humane Italian colonialism" conceals
- After 1936 defeat (Battle of Maychew), the Ethiopian state collapsed — but the resistance mutated rather than ended

## The Arbegnoch (Ethiopian Patriots) and the Zeraf Tradition

- **Shifta/Arbegnoch**: not simple bandits — **social rebels** who refuse unjust authority, abandon the village, and take to the hills; deep roots in Ethiopian culture (the *zeraf* tradition)
- Resistance became **decentralised guerrilla** — impossible to eradicate because it had no single command structure
- Thousands of peasants, ex-soldiers, and local aristocrats armed themselves and conducted ongoing operations against Italian lines of communication

## Women's Resistance: The "Patriots in the Shadows"

- Research (Tessfa Abbao & Gigoo, "The Overlooked Role of Women in the Patriotic Resistance Movement in Burda Mott"):
- While men fought in the mountains, women built the **Ievusta Arbenna** ("Patriote Ombra" / Patriots in the Shadows) network
- Exploited Italian gender prejudice (assuming women docile) to infiltrate markets, bars, military kitchens, colonial offices
- Roles: intelligence gathering, food contraband to rebel camps, weapons smuggling under traditional dress past fascist checkpoints
- **Poetic warfare**: women composed *funfan* (incitement poetry) publicly shaming men who stayed in villages, driving them to join the fighters by social pressure
  - Example figure: Woizero Sindu Yimer — her poetry served "to cure cowardice"
  - Poetry as recruitment, identity construction, traitor stigmatisation — the full spectrum of psychological warfare
- The entire Ethiopian society was mobilised: the peasant became a guerrilla, the market seller became a spy, the poet became an agitator

## The Post-War Betrayal Pattern

- Madagascar, 1947: barely two years after VE Day, France suppressed a revolt with a repression causing tens of thousands of deaths — the same France that had just celebrated its own liberation
- The **militarisation of memory**: the West celebrated its liberation using African weapons, resources, and bodies — then actively denied those same peoples their freedom
- "Legitimacy laundering": Allied victory provided European colonial powers renewed moral authority to crush liberation movements

## The Rethinking Resistance Framework

- Academic framing (*Rethinking Resistance*): African resistance has been read only as reaction to European presence — not as autonomous political action
- The revision: African peoples interpreted the global conflict as **an opportunity for their own liberation** — not a reflex, but a strategic reading
- Multiple resistances, layered and interdependent: rejection of external fascism + rejection of domestic colonial oppression; these are not sequential but simultaneous
- The Eurocentric model of resistance (single leader, clear structure, pitched battles) fails to capture the decentralised, multi-actor, culturally embedded resistance forms

## Why Memory Matters Now

- "The resistance begins with precision" (Tricontinental) — seeing clearly who paid the real price for the freedom enjoyed today
- Colonial extraction logics have not disappeared in 1945 — they evolved: international debt regimes, resource extraction, militarised borders
- Decolonising memory = first step to understanding the world inherited; the asymmetries of the present are legible only through this history
- Tricontinental: "the weapon of memory" — the manipulative use of history to justify present asymmetries; counter-memory as political tool

## Key Takeaways

- Africa was not the periphery of the "Second World War" but its material engine — uranium, food, soldiers — without which Allied victory was impossible
- The Thiaroye massacre and the Algerian 8 May massacres demonstrate that fascist logic survived the European defeat of fascism, operating intact in the colonies
- The Ethiopian resistance (1935–1941) was the first organised global anti-fascist resistance, predating the European fronts — fought through guerrilla, intelligence networks, and poetry
- Ethiopian women's resistance network (Patriote Ombra) is among the most sophisticated documented cases of total-society mobilisation against occupiers
- The "Italiani brava gente" myth is directly contradicted by documented use of poison gas, mass executions, and targeted massacre of civilian and religious communities
- Reclaiming this history is a political act: the asymmetries of the present global order (debt, extraction, military borders) are continuous with colonial extraction, not separate from it
