# Trump, NATO, and European Defence Realignment (2026)

*Sources: Stars and Stripes, German Marshall Fund, Northeastern University, GMF — compiled April 2026.*

## The Pressure Campaign

- Trump demanded NATO members raise defence spending to **5% of GDP** (from the 2% baseline agreed under Obama)
- Result: European NATO members now spending **>$450B/year** — nearly double 2022 levels
- New official NATO targets: **5% of GDP by 2035** for core defence + security-related spending; **3.5%** for core defence requirements alone
- Leading edge: Poland (4.12%, targeting 4.7%); Estonia pledged "at least 5% from 2026"; Lithuania 5–6% by 2026

## Coercive Tactics

- Trump threatened **tariffs on eight NATO allies** participating in joint Greenland military exercises (10% immediately, 25% by June)
- **January 2026**: US increased pressure on Denmark to cede Greenland control — NATO ally treated as target of predatory doctrine
- **April 2026**: Trump administration eyeing moves of European US bases amid deepening NATO rift
- NATO command restructuring: US officials proposing "**NATO 3.0**" — Europeans take the lead; US retains a role but steps back from operational command

## European Strategic Autonomy Response

- European perception of US retrenchment has **accelerated** European defence reinvestment — the pressure campaign is producing the opposite of dependency
- Projected **60% increase** in defence spending in major European markets by 2030
- EU developing contingency plans for US withdrawal from NATO (see also [[eu-role-in-middle-east]] for Hormuz planning)
- EU post-war Hormuz planning explicitly excludes both the US and Israel — European strategic autonomy hardening in real operations
- NATO Secretary General Rutte: "a NATO less dependent on the US is not necessarily a bad thing"

## The Doctrine Connection

- Same **predatory logic** as the Venezuela blitz (see [[predatory-world-order]]): apply maximum pressure; targets either capitulate (spending increases) or face coercive penalties (tariffs)
- But NATO allies are **not Venezuela** — allied democracies can reorient rather than submit, building alternative structures that reduce future US leverage
- **Long-run strategic irony**: Trump's predatory tactics toward allies may accelerate the very European autonomy that reduces US structural leverage over the alliance
- The Machiavellian fox-and-lion strategy requires sufficiently overawed targets — European democracies can invest their way out of dependency

## The Greenland Gambit

- January 2026 Greenland pressure: a strategic asset play — Arctic shipping lanes, rare earth deposits, military positioning
- Logic: apply predatory doctrine to allied territory as well as adversary territory — "annihilate or caress" logic applied to Denmark
- Outcome: Denmark and allies hardened position; EU began accelerating Arctic cooperation frameworks
- Mirrors the rare earth dynamic with China: coercive seizure of strategic chokepoints is the predatory doctrine's operational form

## Key Takeaways

- Trump's 5% GDP demand has dramatically increased European defence spending — the pressure campaign has worked in extracting resources from allies
- But the method has damaged trust and accelerated European strategic autonomy — a long-run strategic loss for US structural influence
- The NATO 3.0 proposal (Europeans lead, US steps back) may become a self-fulfilling prophecy: US retrenches, Europe builds independent capacity, dependency dissolves
- The Greenland gambit reveals the predatory doctrine has no ally exception — all relationships are transactional, all assets potentially contestable
- European contingency planning (Hormuz exclusion of US/Israel; EU defence integration) signals that allies are internalising the retrenchment as permanent, not as negotiating posture
