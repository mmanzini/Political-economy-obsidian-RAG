# Urea and Food Crisis

The energy crisis is metamorphosing into a food crisis through a critical dependency: natural gas → urea → nitrogen fertilisers → food production.

## The Urea Chain

- Natural gas is 60–90% of urea production costs
- The Persian Gulf supplies 35% of global urea exports
- Urea is the basis of nitrogen fertilisers essential for rice, corn, and wheat in Asia
- By 2026-03-31, urea prices had risen **83% since start of year**

## Country Exposure

- **Thailand**: relies on the Middle East for 55% of nitrogen-based raw material imports; fertiliser reserves last until August
- **Indonesia**: Pupuk (state-owned) produces urea domestically, but blending companies have only 45 days of reserves; paradox — producers tempted to export for cash rather than supply domestically
- **India**: imports 40% of urea needs from the Middle East; had hoped for Chinese supplies
- **Philippines**: national state of emergency declared; LPG prices surged from 1,000 to 1,400 pesos, crushing small food vendors ("carinderias" employing ~1M workers)

## China's Role

- Halted all fertiliser exports (~40M tons to stay within Chinese borders)
- Accumulated 3–4 months of oil reserves
- Protects domestic food security while tightening the noose on the rest of Asia
- International urea prices up 40% partly due to Chinese export ban

## Key Takeaways

- Gas → urea → fertiliser → food: the Hormuz blockade is a systemic trap that hits agriculture
- If fertilisers don't arrive in time for planting, entire harvests are lost — not just a price problem
- China's export ban on fertilisers is a strategic move that worsens the crisis for the rest of Asia
- Multiple countries have reserves measured in weeks, not months
