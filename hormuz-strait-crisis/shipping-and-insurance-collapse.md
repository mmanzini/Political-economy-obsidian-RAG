# Shipping and Insurance Collapse

The Hormuz blockade has split the global shipping fleet in two and made conventional maritime insurance economically unviable.

## Insurance Market Freeze

- War risk premiums surged from ~0.125% of hull value to 10%
- For a large oil tanker (hull value ~$138M), a single voyage premium is now $10–14M
- This makes transport economically unsustainable for conventional international fleets

## Fleet Bifurcation

- **Shadow fleet**: vessels under Russian or Iranian protection continue moving toward Tehran-friendly nations
- **Rest of the world**: forced to circumnavigate Africa via the Cape of Good Hope
- The detour adds ~14 days per voyage, with fuel and logistics costs passed to end consumers

## Global Supply Loss

- By 2026-03-31, global petroleum supply had lost ~11 million barrels/day
- Non-OPEC+ producers (US, Brazil) already at maximum technical capacity — cannot fill the gap
- IEA released 400 million barrels from strategic reserves on March 11 — tempered futures but did not resolve physical shortage of refined products

## Key Takeaways

- Insurance premiums ($10–14M per voyage) have made conventional Hormuz transit economically impossible
- The global fleet has split into a shadow fleet (Russian/Iranian protection) and a conventional fleet detouring via Cape of Good Hope (+14 days)
- Physical supply loss of ~11M barrels/day cannot be offset by non-OPEC+ spare capacity
- IEA strategic reserve release was a financial tool, not a physical supply fix
