# MAGA as New Religious Movement: Grace vs Sword

*Source: "Fuori da Qui" podcast Ep.102 — "La grazia e la spada" (Simone Pirani, Coranews), 2026-04-19. Drawing on Vox (Jun 2025, "Donald Trump Is Building a Strange New Religious Movement"), Aeon (Apr 2026, "Revolutionary Tolerance"), The New Yorker ("The Politics of Faith after Charlie Kirk"), and The Atlantic ("Fully MAGA").*

## Thesis: A Rupture with the Old Religious Right

- For six decades the US religious right was a traditional **Christian nationalism** — theocratic in inspiration, trying to conform state law to scriptural standards (prayer in schools, National Prayer Breakfast, etc.)
- The **MAGA religious movement** is **fundamentally different and opposed** to the old religious right
- Traditional model: "state conforms to church"
- MAGA model: **"church is bent to the will of the nation"** — religion subordinated to an ethno-nationalist project
- Comparable structurally to **post-revolutionary Iran** (clerical guardians govern) — but inverted — or to the Russian Orthodox Church under the Kremlin, acting as propaganda and intelligence arm
- Coherence is **political, not theological**

## Institutional Marker: Commission on Religious Freedom

- Previous administrations (all three pre-Trump) maintained an **Office for Faith-Based Partnerships** — treated religious organisations as charitable partners against poverty, human trafficking, etc. (church helping state with collective moral failures)
- Trump admin **abolished this office** and replaced it with a **Commission on Religious Freedom** tasked with producing an official account of religious freedom in America
- Shift: religion is no longer a balm for moral ills; **American history and institutions become the vehicle through which religion sustains itself**
- Religious institutions prosper in proportion to how "sufficiently American" they are — per MAGA definition: **white, heterosexual, unconditionally loyal to the nation**
- "Religious freedom" is redeployed as a weapon — same move the segregationists made to block racial equality — now against LGBTQ+ rights and reproductive healthcare

## The Interreligious Coalition

- Commission dominated by **evangelicals** — emphasis on personal salvation, biblical literalism, emotional worship; appeals to less-educated, less-affluent Americans; naturally more politically conservative than mainline Protestants
- Joined by **conservative Catholics, Orthodox Jews**, a Greek Orthodox archbishop, Dr. Ben Carson (Seventh-day Adventist)
- Notably, two of the three Muslims Trump appointed are **white American converts**
- United not by theology but by a **shared sense of cultural siege** — primarily on gender, sexuality, race
- Willing to subordinate theological concerns entirely to the nation-building project

## Characteristics of the New Religion

- **Total disinterest in the afterlife** — unlike predecessors obsessed with eternal punishment; new leaders use theological language only for cultural influence
- **Pronatalism** as central concern: focus on birth rates, even in abortion discussions — compared by Vox to the **Roman imperial cult**, where religion focused on national power and fertility
- **State celebrations**: parades, new holidays, hagiographic history mandated by the Commission
- Net effect: replacing the trappings of religion with **"America First" alternatives**
- Not traditionalist but radically **transformative** — the goal is to reshape American society in unprecedented ways (the opposite of being conservative)

## Historical Parallel: Castellio vs Calvin (1553)

- 16th century: the age of religious executions, witch hunts, heretic-burnings
- **Calvin** in Geneva defended the execution of theologian **Michael Servetus** (burned 1553 for denying the Trinity) — argued heretics must be "cut down by the sword of the state" to prevent "deadly poison" from spreading; doctrine justifies every violence
- **Sébastien Castellio** — French peasant's son turned Greek professor at Basel — opposed Servetus's execution
- Under pseudonyms he published *Concerning Heretics, Whether They Are to Be Persecuted*: executions for heresy are **not merely cruel but un-Christian** — "Satan could devise nothing more repugnant to Christ's will"
- Castellio's gentle, merciful Christ vs. Calvin's avenging Christ — a **modern conception of Jesus** emerges here
- Recurring civilisational choice between a **Christ of mercy** and a **Christ who brandishes the sword**

## Charlie Kirk: Warrior Theology

- Charlie Kirk: MAGA evangelical activist, assassinated weeks before the podcast
- Self-described mission: "a fighter and an avenger of the right"
- When challenged on his aggressive debate tactics, Kirk defended himself by invoking the **"dual formula"**: "Christ is all grace and all truth"
- His identity: a **warrior**; his calling was not to heal the sick or mend broken marriages but to **combat evil and proclaim truth**
- The New Yorker introduces the counterpoint: **Dallas Willard**, an evangelical thinker who warned believers against "an antagonistic and arrogant spirit in defence of faith" — the apology must be **characterised by gentleness**, because Jesus is a gentle and loving shepherd
- Structural problem: the attention economy **rewards indignation and crushes gentleness** — Turning Point USA posted ~$100M in revenue on Kirk's confrontational debate format

## The Funeral: Grace vs Sword Enacted

- Kirk's memorial service, per The Atlantic, dramatised the grace-vs-sword tension and exposed the movement's **moral hypocrisy**
- **Act 1 — pure grace**: widow **Erika Kirk** invoked Luke 23:34 ("Father forgive them, for they know not what they do"), publicly forgave her husband's assassin: *"The answer to hate is not hate. The answer is love — always love. Love for our enemies and love for those who persecute us."*
- **Act 2 — the sword**: **Trump** eulogised Kirk, then broke from his prepared script: *"This is where I disagreed with Charlie — **I hate my opponents and I don't want the best for them**."*
- The audience **laughed and applauded** — celebrating the "dark passion of hate"
- Trump in a single sentence repudiated the foundational precepts of the faith his supporters profess

## The Atlantic's Diagnosis

- Core question: why do people who claim their greatest desire is to follow Jesus venerate a man like Trump and seem willing to follow him "to the ends of the earth"?
- Diagnosis: **politics fills the void left by faith**
  - Millions of Christians attend churches where pastors preach "love your enemy" and "turn the other cheek"
  - But their **lived weekly reality** is echo chambers and "conflict entrepreneurs" who inflame emotions, leading them to feel the same hatred Trump feels for his enemies
- Christianity was supposed to produce interior transformation — it has **failed** at this
- Culture-war politics offers what the churches no longer do: **a sense of community, a common enemy, scope and meaning** — a grand drama of good vs evil
- Result: the **"consecration of resentment"** — a deformation of Jesus' message, allowing believers to feel at home in "the world of moral ugliness"
- They see themselves as **"theological warriors" / "theobros"** — loving Trump precisely for his **lack of empathy and insatiable desire for revenge**
- These followers "move effortlessly between the two worlds — between grace and the sword"

## Italian Echoes

- The Italian receivers: **Meloni** ("I am a woman, I am a mother, I am Italian, I am Christian"; consecrating Italy to the Immaculate Heart of Mary) and **Salvini** (religious invocations tied to electoral victory)
- A "tatters-of-empire" aesthetic — softer than MAGA but with similar structural moves: religious identity deployed for political mobilisation rather than theological conviction
- Italy as the perennial political laboratory — in this case, receiving and refracting MAGA's religious-nationalist template

## How to Counter the Movement (Vox)

- Old tactic against the old religious right: **expose hypocrisy** — apply their own moral standards to them. It worked — many 80s/90s figures were shamed out of public life
- **MAGA is largely impermeable to shame** — can't be countered by appeals to theological humility, counter-scripture, or conscience
- What drives MAGA is **political utility**, not conscience
- The unlikely allies: **traditionalist conservative believers** — they remain bound by a basic moral architecture that constrains even their leaders, and by a God who cannot ultimately be manipulated
- Frictions already visible:
  - Draconian immigration policy unsettles evangelicals concerned about co-religionists fleeing real persecution
  - Trump's pronatalist **IVF endorsement** disturbs Catholic and conservative Christian constituencies
- Strategic conclusion: "the enemy of your enemy might be your political frenemy"
- Trump/MAGA has declared religious war **not only on secularism and progressive faith, but on traditional religion that refuses to serve their radical worldview**
- This is **not** a coming theocracy; **not** *The Handmaid's Tale* — it is something new, stranger, harder to fight
- A **religion of nation and identity** masquerading in the trappings of familiar faiths
- Can only be defeated by **naming and unmasking it**, and by unexpected alliances with believers who — whatever their doctrine — still believe in a power higher than Donald Trump

## Key Takeaways

- The MAGA religious movement is not a continuation of the old Christian right but a rupture: religion subordinated to nationalism, not vice versa
- The institutional marker is the replacement of the Office for Faith-Based Partnerships with the Commission on Religious Freedom — religion as state agiography rather than social-service partner
- Baudrillard-adjacent structural move: the form of Christianity is preserved while its substance (grace, forgiveness, love of enemies) is hollowed out and replaced with nationalist content
- The Kirk funeral — Erika Kirk's Luke 23:34 forgiveness vs Trump's "I hate my opponents" laugh-line — is the exemplary tableau of this substitution
- Castellio vs Calvin is the deep template: every Christian civilisation re-fights the choice between a Christ of mercy and a Christ of the sword
- The movement is shame-proof, so the standard counter-tactic (exposing hypocrisy) fails; the remaining lever is coalition with traditionalist believers who still recognise a moral authority higher than the political leader
- Italian politicians (Meloni, Salvini) are importing a softer version of the same template — religious identity as political mobilisation tool
