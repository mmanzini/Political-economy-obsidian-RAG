## Forecasts

- Goldman Sachs: if the Strait remains closed another month, average Brent for 2026 could reach $100
- Asian diesel and jet fuel benchmarks have doubled, exceeding $200/barrel in some cases

## Volatility

- Extremely volatile sessions driven by news from Israel/Lebanon and Hormuz
- Traders chasing headlines; prices swing several dollars intraday
- Weekly losses in double digits for WTI despite intraday spikes

## Key Takeaways

- Brent has risen ~40% from pre-war levels (~$70 to ~$96)
- North Sea oil at all-time highs above 2022 peaks
- Extreme intraday volatility makes positioning difficult
- Physical scarcity of refined products is driving Asian benchmarks beyond crude prices
