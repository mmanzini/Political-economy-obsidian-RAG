# LNG Crisis and Gas Markets

## LNG Supply Collapse

- Qatar exports 93% of its LNG through Hormuz — production suspended 2026-03-02, force majeure declared 2026-03-04
- Ras Laffan (93% of Qatar's LNG output) attacked — 17% capacity damaged, repair up to 5 years
- Work has resumed at Ras Laffan but full operations are far off
- Asian LNG prices surged **127% in 30 days**

## European Gas (TTF)

- TTF up 2.5% at €46.44/MWh
- Driven by Hormuz closure and supply uncertainty

## Hormuz Impact on Gas

- 90% of LNG transiting Hormuz is destined for Asian markets
- Physical scarcity, not just price — gas is needed for electricity, heating, chips manufacturing, and fertiliser production
- Without gas, the knock-on effects hit food supply (urea/fertiliser production)

## Key Takeaways

- Qatar's LNG force majeure is a direct threat to Japan, South Korea, and other Asian importers
- 127% price surge in 30 days — the most violent reaction among energy markets
- Damage to Ras Laffan may take years to repair, creating a structural gap
- Gas scarcity has cascading effects: energy → industry → food (via fertilisers)
