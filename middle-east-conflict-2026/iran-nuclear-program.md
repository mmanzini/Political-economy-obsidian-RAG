# Iran's Nuclear Program

*Stub — this topic is the central sticking point in ceasefire negotiations. Expand as source material becomes available.*

## Current Status
- Iran has continued enriching uranium throughout the 2026 conflict (source: needs-verification)
- The US killed Khamenei but did not achieve regime change; the new leadership has taken a harder line on the nuclear file (source: needs-verification)
- Iran's position in ceasefire talks: the nuclear program is non-negotiable; it will not surrender enriched uranium as a condition of peace (source: needs-verification)
- Trump claimed early in the ceasefire that "the US would take Iran's enriched uranium" — this proved false; Iran denied it (source: needs-verification)

## Historical Context
- Netanyahu proposed to Trump, Obama, Bush, and Biden that killing Khamenei would trigger regime collapse and resolve the nuclear issue — all previous presidents rejected the proposal (source: needs-verification)
- The Trump administration acted on this logic in late February 2026 (*Operation Epic Fury*); the result was the opposite: a younger, more radical leadership took over with no incentive to negotiate (source: needs-verification)
- The Saudis have reportedly proposed a **20-year freeze on Iran's nuclear activities** as part of a broader settlement framework (source: needs-verification)

## Why It Matters
- The nuclear program is structurally irreducible in negotiations: Iran sees it as sovereign deterrence; Israel sees any Iranian nuclear capability as an existential threat; the US cannot credibly claim victory without addressing it (source: needs-verification)
- US waiver on Iranian oil exports expired **19 April 2026** — used partly as leverage on the nuclear file (source: needs-verification)
- Without a nuclear agreement, any ceasefire is a pause, not a settlement (source: needs-verification)

## Key Takeaways
- The nuclear program is the hardest node in the conflict — neither side can yield without losing core credibility
- Iran's new leadership is less pragmatic than Khamenei on this issue, not more
- Saudi 20-year freeze proposal is the most concrete framework on the table

## Summary

Iran's nuclear program is the central, structurally irreducible obstacle in the 2026 Middle East conflict negotiations. Following the US killing of Khamenei in Operation Epic Fury, Iran's new, more hardline leadership has made the nuclear program non-negotiable, while the Saudis have proposed a 20-year freeze as the most concrete framework on the table. Without a nuclear agreement, any ceasefire remains a pause rather than a settlement.
