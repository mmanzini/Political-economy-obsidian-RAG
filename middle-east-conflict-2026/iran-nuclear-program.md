# Iran's Nuclear Program

*Stub — this topic is the central sticking point in ceasefire negotiations. Expand as source material becomes available.*

## Current Status
- Iran has continued enriching uranium throughout the 2026 conflict
- The US killed Khamenei but did not achieve regime change; the new leadership has taken a harder line on the nuclear file
- Iran's position in ceasefire talks: the nuclear program is non-negotiable; it will not surrender enriched uranium as a condition of peace
- Trump claimed early in the ceasefire that "the US would take Iran's enriched uranium" — this proved false; Iran denied it

## Historical Context
- Netanyahu proposed to Trump, Obama, Bush, and Biden that killing Khamenei would trigger regime collapse and resolve the nuclear issue — all previous presidents rejected the proposal
- The Trump administration acted on this logic in late February 2026 (*Operation Epic Fury*); the result was the opposite: a younger, more radical leadership took over with no incentive to negotiate
- The Saudis have reportedly proposed a **20-year freeze on Iran's nuclear activities** as part of a broader settlement framework

## Why It Matters
- The nuclear program is structurally irreducible in negotiations: Iran sees it as sovereign deterrence; Israel sees any Iranian nuclear capability as an existential threat; the US cannot credibly claim victory without addressing it
- US waiver on Iranian oil exports expired **19 April 2026** — used partly as leverage on the nuclear file
- Without a nuclear agreement, any ceasefire is a pause, not a settlement

## Key Takeaways
- The nuclear program is the hardest node in the conflict — neither side can yield without losing core credibility
- Iran's new leadership is less pragmatic than Khamenei on this issue, not more
- Saudi 20-year freeze proposal is the most concrete framework on the table
