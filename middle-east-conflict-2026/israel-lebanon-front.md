## Key Takeaways
- The Israel-Lebanon front is the most likely trigger for ceasefire collapse
- Israel and the US have diverging objectives: Netanyahu wants regime change; the US wants an exit
- Direct Israel-Lebanon talks are the diplomatic track most likely to reduce immediate risk
- Any comprehensive settlement must address this front or the ceasefire remains fragile

## Summary

The Israel-Lebanon front is the primary near-term threat to the 2026 Iran-US ceasefire. Israel's continued strikes in Lebanon on Day 1 of the ceasefire period, combined with diverging US-Israeli objectives and Iran's declared position that Lebanon is an inseparable part of any ceasefire, make this front the most likely trigger for renewed hostilities. Direct Israel-Lebanon talks are the key diplomatic track to watch.
