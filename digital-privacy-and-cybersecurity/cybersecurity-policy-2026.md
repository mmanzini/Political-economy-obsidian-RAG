# Cybersecurity Policy Landscape 2026

*Sources: Coalfire, Forcepoint, Perspective Labs, Detecon, AI Magicx, Just Security — compiled April 2026.*

## EU AI Act: Key Enforcement Milestones

- **2 August 2026**: core framework broadly operational; Annex III high-risk provisions take full effect
- **August 2027**: second wave of provisions
- High-risk AI categories (Annex III) — mandatory conformity assessments before EU deployment:
  - Hiring and employment screening
  - Credit scoring and biometrics
  - Critical infrastructure, education, law enforcement, border control
- **Article 15 (cybersecurity requirements)**: accuracy, robustness, and resilience mandatory against adversarial attacks — prompt injection, data poisoning, model extraction
- Extraterritorial scope: non-EU companies must comply if deploying AI in the EU or if outputs are used in the EU — same logic as GDPR
- Penalties: up to €35M or 7% of global annual revenue; enforcement by national regulators

## Data Sovereignty and the AI Processing Gap

- At least 34 countries have enacted data localisation requirements restricting where AI processing can occur
- 29% of organisations cite cross-border AI transfers as their top compliance exposure
- The critical gap: most organisations have solved sovereignty for **storage** but not for where data is **processed, trained, or inferred**
- EU-US data privacy framework collapse creates a live regulatory exposure: API calls to US-hosted AI models may violate regulations in 30+ countries
- 2026 transition: from large language models to **agentic AI** (autonomous reasoning, real-world execution) — raises the stakes dramatically
  - Agentic systems require vast, low-latency compute to manage supply chains, cyber operations, and financial markets
  - The underlying hardware becomes physical infrastructure of sovereignty
  - Export control implication: controlling agentic AI infrastructure = controlling a nation's strategic capacity

## Trump Administration Cybersecurity Strategy

- January 2026: White House issued new cybersecurity strategy
- Heavy emphasis on **offensive cyber and deterrence** — departure from Biden-era defensive posture
- Signals willingness to use cyber capabilities as instruments of coercion, mirroring the predatory doctrine in the physical domain (see [[predatory-world-order]])
- AI chip export controls weaponised as coercive lever in the US-China trade war (see [[trump-china-trade-war]])

## EU vs US Governance Comparison

| Dimension | EU Approach | US Approach (Trump) |
|-----------|-------------|---------------------|
| AI governance | Rules-based, risk classification, mandatory conformity | Deregulatory; offensive cyber emphasis |
| Data sovereignty | Localisation + GDPR extraterritoriality | Bilateral deals; CLOUD Act access |
| Export controls | Dual-use regulation (Wassenaar Arrangement) | Weaponised as trade/sanctions tool |
| Platform liability | Digital Services Act | Rollback of Section 230 reforms |

## Platform Economy and Health Data

- Platform economy logic applied to healthcare: data extracted as by-product of service delivery (see [[vastaamo-data-breach]])
- Health data sovereignty problem: patient is EU-based, platform is US-based, AI processing is in Singapore — no regulation covers the full chain cleanly
- EU AI Act covers risk classification but not the ownership/monetisation layer — a structural gap
- Uberisation logic applied to health data: the intermediary diffuses responsibility, accumulating sovereign-level data without sovereign-level accountability

## Key Takeaways

- The EU AI Act August 2026 deadline creates concrete compliance obligations for any company deploying AI systems that affect European residents — not just EU-based firms
- The real sovereignty gap is not storage (largely solved) but processing and inference — agentic AI will widen this gap dramatically
- Trump's January 2026 cybersecurity strategy signals a shift from defensive to offensive cyber posture, mirroring the broader predatory doctrine
- The EU-US divide on digital governance is structurally deepening — companies operating across both jurisdictions face irreconcilable regulatory pressures
- Agentic AI infrastructure is becoming the new contested terrain of economic sovereignty, equivalent to critical energy infrastructure
