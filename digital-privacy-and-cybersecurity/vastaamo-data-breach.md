# Vastaamo Data Breach: The Uberisation of Mental Health

## The Attack (2020)

- **Vastaamo**, Finland's largest private psychotherapy network, was hacked — ~**33,000 patient records** stolen
- The database had **no firewall**, was internet-accessible, and the admin password was **empty** (just press Enter)
- Patients received extortion emails: pay 200 bitcoin or have therapy transcripts (divorces, trauma, suicide attempts, abuse) published online
- First 100 records published on the dark web included politicians, police officers, public figures
- A search engine was later created allowing anyone to look up their neighbours' therapy records

## The Hacker: Aleksanteri Kivimaki ("Zeekill")

- Finnish, 28 years old at the time of arrest; began hacking career as a teenager
- History of **swatting** (sending armed police to victims' homes via fake emergency calls) and online harassment
- 2014: grounded the plane of the Sony Online Entertainment president with a fake bomb threat; took down PlayStation and Xbox servers at Christmas
- Arrogance was his downfall: accidentally uploaded his own personal folder to the dark web while automating the data release
- Police traced credit card payments (OnlyFans, Apple services) back to him
- Arrested in Paris in February 2023 during a domestic violence call, carrying a fake Romanian passport
- Trial: **21,000 plaintiffs** — broadcast in cinemas because no courtroom could hold them all
- Sentenced to **6 years and 3 months**

## Systemic Failure: The Uberisation of Care

- Vastaamo was founded as a **startup** aiming to be the "McDonald's of therapy" — fast, digital, scalable
- In 2019, a private equity fund acquired majority ownership; founder cashed out €5M+
- Priority was growth and efficiency, not security — "if you treat therapy like pizza delivery, you'll build the security infrastructure of a pizzeria"
- **Digital Taylorism**: standardising therapeutic procedures, reducing care to tasks, optimising for speed and cost
- CEO **Ville Tapio** knew about the breach for weeks before patients were contacted; initially convicted of criminal negligence (acquitted on appeal)
- The "Uber model" diffuses responsibility: platform is an intermediary, therapists are providers, patients are users, data is an asset

## Historical Parallel

- The **Berners Street Hoax** (London, 1810): prankster Theodore Hook sent 4,000 fake letters summoning merchants, dignitaries, and the mayor to one address — the 19th-century version of swatting
- Modern swatters hack webcams and smart doorbells to watch the chaos remotely
- 2017: **Andrew Finch** shot dead by police in a swatting incident originating from a $1.50 video game argument

## Impact

- Thousands of Finns have **stopped seeking therapy** out of fear, destroying trust in the care system
- The damage is not just individual but institutional — to the very concept of digital care
- Finland, "the most digital nation in Europe," demonstrated that digital advancement without security investment is a trap

## Key Takeaways

- The Vastaamo breach exposed 33,000 therapy records because a startup prioritised growth over basic security (no firewall, empty password)
- The case exemplifies the dangers of applying platform-economy logic ("uberisation") to sensitive healthcare
- Trust destruction extends far beyond victims — it deters an entire population from seeking mental health support
- Swatting and data extortion represent the militarisation of personal information for entertainment and profit
