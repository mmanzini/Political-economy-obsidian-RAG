# Anthropic Growth — Amol Avasare Takeaways

Lenny Rachitsky's summary of a conversation with Amol Avasare, Head of Growth at Anthropic (April 2026).

## The PM/Eng Ratio Compression

- Claude Code makes a 5-engineer team produce the output of 15–20.
- PM and design productivity have **not** scaled proportionally → one PM now manages output of a much larger eng team.
- Anthropic's response: **hire more PMs** *and* deputise product-minded engineers as mini-PMs for any project under two weeks of eng time.

## CASH — Claude Accelerates Sustainable Hypergrowth

Internal initiative using Claude to automate growth work itself across four stages: identify opportunities → build features → test quality → analyse results. Currently handles copy and minor UI tweaks; win rate comparable to a junior PM with 2–3 years of experience, improving rapidly.

## What AI Can't Automate: Alignment

- "Even with AGI, it'll still be impossible to align six stakeholders."
- Cross-functional coordination, politics, mediating tradeoffs → the bottleneck AI doesn't touch on larger projects.
- This is why PM roles aren't going away, and may grow.

## Death of the PRD

- **60–80% of growth team projects have no PRD.**
- Small work: Slack kickoffs with product-minded engineers who can push back.
- Larger work: a 30-minute cross-functional kickoff (legal, safeguards, stakeholders) to surface concerns early.

## Onboarding Friction

Counterintuitive: adding friction to onboarding **drives** growth — *if* it helps users understand why the product is for them. Cut annoying friction, add clarifying friction.

## Bigger Bets, Not Better A/B Tests

- Traditional growth: 60–70% small optimisations, 20–30% big swings.
- At Anthropic this ratio is flipped.
- Logic: if your value is AI-driven, future value is orders of magnitude higher than today's, so micro-optimisations capture a shrinking share of a growing pie.

## Personal Tooling

Amol built a weekly AI agent that scans Slack (via Cowork + Slack MCP) for cross-functional misalignment, surfacing teams about to do overlapping work. Already caught major misalignment that would have wasted weeks.

## Key Takeaways

- The PM compression problem and the alignment problem are two sides of the same coin: execution scaled, but coordination didn't, and coordination has nowhere to live except Slack threads and meetings that evaporate.
- AI-native growth teams should **flip** the optimisation/big-bet ratio.
- The PRD is dying; what replaces it must encode intent crisply enough for both humans to align on and agents to execute from.
