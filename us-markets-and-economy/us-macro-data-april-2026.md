# US Macro Data — April 2026

## Inflation

- March CPI expected at **3.3%** (up from 2.4%) — largest monthly increase since 2022
- Energy projected to contribute +10.6%
- Core inflation expected at 2.7% (up from 2.5%)
- PCE price index: +0.4% in February (in line with expectations)

## GDP

- Q4 GDP steady at 0.5% (Q3 was 4.4%)
- Consumption slowing: 3.5% → 1.9%
- Private investment rose 2.3%
- Longest government shutdown in history occurred in Q4

## Labour Market

- Initial jobless claims at two-year lows

## Fed and Rates

- 10-year Treasury yield: 4.29% (staying below 4.3%)
- 30-year bond auction: yield up 0.5% vs. last issuance
- Rate cut probability by year-end: dropped from 56% to **30%** in two days
- Dollar index heading for weekly decline of ~1.5%
- Euro/dollar below 1.17

## Fed Leadership

- Senate hearing on Kevin Warsh postponed (paperwork issues)
- Senator Tom Tillis refuses to vote until DOJ case against Jerome Powell is resolved

## Consumer Confidence

- University of Michigan preliminary: expected 52.9 (down from 53)

## IMF (Spring Meetings Preview)

- Kristalina Georgieva: global growth forecasts to be cut (was projected at 3.3%)
- Warned governments: only use targeted/temporary support if fiscal space exists
- Avoid deficit-increasing measures that fuel inflation
- Central banks advised to wait before intervening

## Key Takeaways

- March CPI expected to show largest monthly jump since 2022 — energy-driven
- Rate cut expectations collapsing (56% → 30%) as inflation rises
- US economy resilient compared to Asia (net exporter advantage) but consumption weakening
- IMF signalling global growth downgrades at Spring Meetings
