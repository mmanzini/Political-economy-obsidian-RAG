# Markets Rally: April 2026

## The "Tale of Two Stories"
- **Financial markets**: rallying on de-escalation signals from the Iran conflict
- **Physical/real economy**: still contracting under energy supply disruption
- Key divergence: Brent futures at ~$95 on peace hopes vs. physical oil above $130/barrel
- Goldman Sachs (Toni Pasquariello): two factors driving S&P resilience — (1) market never lost faith in US economic fundamentals; (2) earnings expectations have risen

## Equity Performance (End of March to Mid-April 2026)
- **Nasdaq**: +14% in 10 trading sessions — best streak since 2021
- **S&P 500**: +10%; one step from an all-time high
- **Dow Jones**: +7%
- Technology sector led the rally; software rebounding strongly
- Largest short-covering in a decade: hedge fund ETF short exposure on US equities fell **11.5%** in one week

## Technology Highlights
- **Nvidia**: +18% in 10 days (best streak since 2023); denied interest in acquiring OHP
- **CoreWeave**: +15% in 2 weeks; extended agreement with Bloom Energy (3GW of power through 2027); exploring equity stake in Bloom → Bloom market cap +$70B in recent weeks
- **Amazon**: near November highs; announced acquisition of **Global Star for ~$11.6B** to compete with Starlink and integrate satellite connectivity with smartphones (Global Star already has an Apple deal)
- **Meta**: +4%; **Microsoft**: +2%
- **OpenAI**: growing investor skepticism over the $852B valuation ahead of a potential IPO; FT analysis: strategy shift toward enterprise (chasing Anthropic) makes the company more vulnerable on two fronts — to Anthropic and to Google's more aggressive enterprise push
- **Anthropic**: VC round reportedly being structured at a valuation near **$800B**; Claude downloads increasing at OpenAI's expense (a direct commercial result of the military AI controversy — see [[silicon-valley-military-ai-split]])

## Banking Earnings Q1 2026 (6th Consecutive Quarter of US Double-Digit Earnings Growth)
- **BlackRock**: $13.9T AUM (slightly below Dec 2025 record of $14T); best quarter ever; record ETF inflows ($130B for iShares); revenues +27% to $6.7B; organic fee growth +8%; net income +11%
- **JPMorgan**: revenues +10%; net income +13%; trading revenues $11.6B (+20% — record); BUT lowered net interest income guidance to **$103B** → stock fell 0.5% despite record results; Dimon: AI and cyber risk are the two main risks today; testing Anthropic's Mitos model
- **Citigroup**: revenues $24.6B (not seen in 10 years); equity trading >$2B (+40%); fixed income +13%; stock +3.2%; **+105% over the past 12 months** under Jane Fraser
- **Wells Fargo**: earnings beat; revenues missed (–5%); private credit exposure $36.2B
- UBS revised S&P 500 EPS growth estimate to **+17%** for 2026 — highest pace in 5 years

## IMF Forecasts (April 2026)
- Global growth revised down to **3.1%** (–0.2pp)
- **Adverse scenario** (oil ~$100, conflict continues): global GDP 2.5%; inflation 5.4%
- **Severe scenario**: GDP 2.0% both 2025 and 2026; inflation >6%
- IMF chief economist: "We are closer to the adverse scenario"
- Stark finding: **50% of fertilizer price increases pass through to food prices within 12 months** — food inflation risk underappreciated
- Italy: +0.5% growth both years; Spain: +2.8% (top G7 performer); UK: 0.8% (most impacted among rich countries)
- IMF warned countries to prepare for **market dysfunction and a liquidity squeeze**

## Fed and Rates
- Janet Yellen (ex-Fed chair): expects **one rate cut in 2026**; called Trump's pressure on the Fed "banana republic behavior"
- Bessent (Treasury): "markets are overreacting" — had earlier walked back a Fed-cut suggestion
- ECB market pricing: 2 rate hikes priced in for 2026; 30% chance of a third

## Key Takeaways
- Markets are front-running a peace deal that has not yet materialized; the real economy hasn't recovered
- Tech leadership and strong banking earnings provide fundamental support, but the paper/physical oil divergence is the main risk asymmetry
- Anthropic's rising valuation and Claude's market share gain vs. OpenAI are a commercial consequence of the military AI controversy
- IMF adverse scenario (now closer to base case) implies persistent inflation and slower growth — not yet priced in
- The fertilizer-food price transmission lag is an underappreciated risk for emerging market stability in late 2026
